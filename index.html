What Makes a Strong Password?

A strong password should meet all of the following criteria:

  • Length: At least 12 characters — longer is always better
  • Uppercase letters: Include at least one capital letter (A–Z)
  • Lowercase letters: Mix in lowercase letters (a–z)
  • Numbers: Add digits (0–9) throughout
  • Special characters: Use symbols like !@#$%^&*()
  • No dictionary words: Avoid common words, names, or dates

Why Password Strength Matters

Weak passwords are the #1 cause of account breaches. A simple 6-character password can be cracked in seconds with modern hardware. A 12-character password mixing letters, numbers, and symbols would take millions of years to crack with brute force — making it effectively unbreakable.

Is my password saved or stored?
No. This tool runs 100% in your browser. Your password never leaves your device and is never transmitted to any server.
What is a "crack time" estimate?
It's an estimate of how long it would take a modern computer using brute-force methods to guess your password. The longer the crack time, the safer your password.
How often should I change my password?
Security experts now recommend changing passwords only when you suspect a breach — but always use a unique, strong password for every account.
What's the best way to manage strong passwords?
Use a reputable password manager (like Bitwarden, 1Password, or Dashlane) to generate and store unique strong passwords for every site.
